# Logic Weise LLC — Website Strategy & SEO Implementation Guide

## Strategic Summary

Logic Weise's new website is architected around one central thesis: **"We solve complex commercial integration and rescue work when other contractors stall out."**

The site is organized to serve four audiences: facility managers, general contractors, property management teams, and commercial project owners. Every page leads toward a consultation request or direct phone contact.

### Key Strategic Decisions

1. **Takeover/rescue as the primary differentiator** — not just a service, but the brand story
2. **Commercial-only positioning** — stated explicitly and repeatedly to filter out residential inquiries
3. **DFW-specific content** — not generic national security copy, but locally relevant language
4. **AI discoverability** — answer-first formatting, extractable summary blocks, FAQ schema, llms.txt
5. **Multi-system coordination** — positioned as single-team accountability vs. subcontractor chaos

## Internal Linking Strategy

### Link Architecture Rules
1. **Homepage** links to all 6 service pages, all 4 industry pages, FAQ, case studies
2. **Service pages** link to related industry pages, related buyer guides, related city pages, and takeover service
3. **Industry pages** link to relevant service pages
4. **Buyer guide pages** link to matching service pages and the contact page
5. **Case studies** link to relevant service pages
6. **FAQ** links to service pages within answers
7. **City pages** link to service pages with city-specific copy
8. **Footer** includes all services, all company pages, and top service areas on every page

### Cross-linking Examples
- Access Control page → Office Security industry page
- Takeover page → "What to Do When an Integrator Abandons" buyer guide
- Fire Alarm page → "Fire Alarm Mistakes That Delay Inspections" buyer guide
- Structured Cabling page → "TI Cabling Checklist" buyer guide
